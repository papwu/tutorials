Q 1 - Which one of the following countries is a leading Cotton producer?

A - China

B - India

C - United States

D - Pakistan

Answer : A

Explanation

China is the largest Cotton producer in the world, followed by India, the US, and Pakistan.

Q 2 - Kimberley is known for ……………

A - Copper

B - Diamond

C - Gold

D - Coal

Answer : B

Explanation

Located in South Africa, Kimberley is known for the diamond mining.

Q 3 - Consider the following statements:

1. A cold air mass has a typical feature i.e. it is colder than the underlying surface and is associated with the instability and atmospheric turbulence.

2. A warm air mass has a typical feature i.e. it is warmer than the underlying surface and is associated with the unstable weather conditions.

Select the correct answer from the given codes:

A - Only 1

B - Only 2

C - Both 1 & 2

D - Neither 1 nor 2

Answer : A

Explanation

A warm air mass has a typical feature i.e. it is warmer than the underlying surface and is associated with the stable weather conditions.

Q 5 - Which one of the following is the deepest point in Australia?

A - Lake Eyre

B - Lake Amadeus

C - Lake Mackay

D - Lake Sylvester

Answer : A

Explanation

Located in the south region of Australia, lake Eyre is the deepest point in Australia.

Q 6 - Consider the following statements:

1. The inner core of this wood is jet black and when it is smoothed, it turns into metallic lusture.

2. It is insect resistant.

3. It is used in ornamental carving and decoration.

Based on the given information, find name of the tree from the given options:

A - Toon

B - Chaplas

C - Telsur

D - Ebony

Answer : D

Explanation

Found in the dry evergreen forest regions of Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Goa, and Maharashtra, Ebony is a valuable tree. Its wood is used in making musical instruments and sports goods.

Q 7 - The Tropical cyclone that affects India, normally develops during the months of …

A - August & September

B - September & October

C - October & November

D - November & December

Answer : C

Explanation

Normally, the ideal time of tropical cyclone formation in India is between October and November.

Q 8 - Which one of the following cities is not the example of a religious city?

A - Ajmer

B - Varanasi

C - Allahabad

D - Vishakhapatnam

Answer : D

Explanation

Vishakhapatnam is an example of industrial city.

Q 9 - Desert soil is predominant in which one of the following regions?

A - North India

B - South India

C - West India

D - East India

Answer : C

Explanation

West India is largely desert (namely Thar Desert); therefore, the entire region is having desert soil.

Q 10 - Columbia Plateau is located in …………………………

A - South USA

B - West USA

C - North-west USA

D - South-west USA

Answer : C

Explanation

Columbia Plateau is also known in Snake plateau.

Q 11 - Consider the following statements:

1. The home of an Eskimo family in Tundra region is known as ‘Igloo.’

2. Igloo is made up of skin and bones of tundra animals.

Select the correct answer from the given codes:

A - Only 1

B - Only 2

C - Both 1 & 2

D - Neither 1 nor 2

Answer : A

Explanation

Igloo is made up of ice.

Q 12 - Which one of the following states leads in cattle farming?

A - Madhya Pradesh

B - Uttar Pradesh

C - Bihar

D - West Bengal

Answer : A

Explanation

Madhya Pradesh has the largest number of cattle population followed by Uttar Pradesh and Bihar.

Q 13 - Which one of the following is popular as ‘Floating Islands Lake?’

A - Nako Lake

B - Loktak Lake

C - Pongong Tso

D - Khajjiar Lake

Answer : B

Explanation

Located in Manipur, Loktak Lake is popular as ‘Floating Islands Lake.’

Q 14 - Which one of the following is the largest delta in the world?

A - Amazon delta

B - Nile delta

C - Ganga-Brahmaputra delta

D - Mississippi-Missouri delta

Answer : C

Explanation

Ganga-Brahmaputra delta (in the Bay of Bengal) is the largest delta in the world.

Q 15 - Dul Hasti Project is constructed on …

A - Beas River

B - Satluj River

C - Jhelum River

D - Chenab River

Answer : D

Explanation

Located in Jammu & Kashmir, Dul Hasti project is constructed on Chenab river.

Q 16 - Because of the heat, coal gets transformed into ………

A - Marble

B - Quartzite

C - Schist

D - Graphite

Answer : D

Explanation

Coal is a Sedimentary rock, but because of the heat, it gets transformed into a graphite rock.

Q 17 - Which one of the following is popular as ‘City of Arabian Nights?’

A - Baghdad

B - Dubai

C - Muscat

D - Doha

Answer : A

Explanation

Baghdad, the capital of Iraq, is popular with its nickname ‘City of Arabian Nights.’

Q 18 - Who has propounded the theory of ‘Continental Drift?’

A - Wegener

B - McKenzie

C - Parker

D - Morgan

Answer : A

Explanation

Alfred Wegener first proposed the Continental Drift Theory in 1910s.

Q 19 - Floriculture is related to ………

A - Farming of flowering and ornamental plants

B - Vegetable farming

C - Farming and management of grapes

D - Farming and management of fruit

Answer : A

Explanation

Floriculture is one of the parts of horticulture concerned with the farming of flowering and ornamental plants.

Q 20 - Which one of the following is the largest producer of copper?

A - Madhya Pradesh

B - Rajasthan

C - Jharkhand

D - Andhra Pradesh

Answer : A

Explanation

With more than 55 percent of the share, Madhya Pradesh is the largest producer of copper in India followed by Rajasthan and Jharkhand.
