Q 4 - The focuses A (1,2) ,B(5,4) ,C(3,8) and D(- 1,6) taken all together are the vertices of

A - A rectangle

B - a rhombus

C - a square

D - none of these

Explanation


AB2= (5-1) 2+ (4-2) 2= (16+4) = 20
BC2= (3-5) 2+ (8-4) 2= (4+16) = 20
CD2= (-1-3) 2+ (6-8) 2= (16+4) = 20

∴ AB= BC= CD= DA= √20
Diagonal AC2= (3-1) 2+ (8-2) 2= (4+36) = 40
Diagonal BD2= (-1-5) 2+ (6-4) 2= (36+4) = 40
∴ Diag AC= Diag BD =√40
∴ ABCD is a square.

Q 5 - The vertices of a triangle are A (3, 8), B (- 4, 2) and C (5, - 1). The region of ∆ ABC is:

A - 57/2 sq. unit

B - 75/2 sq. unit

C - 57 sq. unit

D - 75 q. units

Answer : B

Explanation

Here x₁=3, x₂= -4, Xᴈ = 5, y₁ =8, y₂= 2 and Yᴈ = -1
 ∆ = 1/2 [x₁(y₂-Yᴈ) +x₂(Yᴈ-y₁) +Xᴈ (y₁-y₂)]
 =1/2 [3(2+1)-4(-1-8) +5(8-2)] = 1/2 (9+36+30) = 75/2 sq.unit.

Q 6 - In what proportion does the point P (2, - 5) separate the line fragment joining A (- 3, 5) and B (4, - 9)?

A - 2:3

B - 4:3

C - 5:1

D - 5:2

Answer : D

Explanation

Let the required ratio be ℏ:1
 Then, the point P is [(4ℏ-3/ℏ+1), (-9ℏ+5/ℏ+1)]
 ∴ 4ℏ-3/ℏ+1 =2,-9ℏ+5/ℏ+1=-5
 ⇒ 4ℏ-3= 2ℏ+2 and -9ℏ+5 = -5ℏ-5
 ⇒ 2ℏ= 5 and 4ℏ=10 ⇒ ℏ= 5/2
 Required ratio is 5/2:1, i.e. 5:2
